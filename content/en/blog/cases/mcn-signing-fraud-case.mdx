# MCN Agency Influencer Signing Fraud Case

An MCN agency was about to sign an influencer claiming $100K monthly income. RealCap verification revealed AI-generated screenshots, preventing a $500K contract fraud.

## Introduction

Multi-Channel Network (MCN) agencies face growing risks from influencers who fabricate their income and engagement metrics. This case study reveals how one agency used RealCap to avoid a costly fraudulent signing.

## The Situation

A mid-sized MCN agency was in negotiations with a promising influencer who claimed:

- **Monthly income**: $100,000+ from multiple platforms
- **Follower count**: 2.5M+ across platforms
- **Engagement rate**: 8-12% consistently

The influencer presented impressive screenshots showing substantial earnings from YouTube, TikTok, and Instagram. The agency was ready to sign a $500,000 annual contract.

## The Red Flags

During the initial review, the talent acquisition team noticed several inconsistencies:

1. **Inconsistent formatting** - Screenshot layouts varied slightly from official platform dashboards
2. **Altered logos** - The platform logos appeared slightly distorted
3. **Mismatched timestamps** - Creation dates in metadata didn't match displayed dates
4. **Pixelation around text** - Signs of digital manipulation in earnings figures

Despite these concerns, the influencer provided additional "verification" screenshots that appeared legitimate at first glance.

## The Verification

The agency decided to run all screenshots through RealCap's verification system before finalizing the contract.

**RealCap's analysis revealed:**

- **AI-generated content detected**: The screenshots showed patterns consistent with AI image generation tools
- **Metadata manipulation**: Exif data showed the images were created the same day they were submitted, contradicting claims of historical earnings
- **Inconsistent fonts**: Typography didn't match official platform fonts
- **Edge artifacts**: Digital manipulation artifacts visible around earnings figures

**Confidence score**: 94% probability of fraudulent content

## The Outcome

Armed with RealCap's verification report, the agency:

- Declined to sign the contract
- Avoided a $500,000 financial loss
- Implemented mandatory RealCap verification for all new signings

### Additional Discovery

After declining, the agency discovered the influencer had previously signed with two other MCNs using the same fraudulent screenshots, resulting in combined losses exceeding $800,000.

## Key Takeaways

1. **Never trust screenshots at face value** - Even convincing images can be AI-generated or manipulated
2. **Implement verification early** - Run verification before contract negotiations begin
3. **Look for patterns** - Multiple inconsistent details often indicate fraud
4. **Use technology** - AI-generated content requires AI-powered detection

## Implementation Recommendations

For MCN agencies looking to protect themselves:

- Integrate RealCap verification into your talent acquisition workflow
- Require screenshot verification before any contract discussion
- Train talent scouts to recognize common manipulation tactics
- Establish clear verification policies for all income/engagement claims
