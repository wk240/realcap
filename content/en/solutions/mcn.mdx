# MCN Agency Screenshot Verification

MCN agencies face increasingly serious income data forgery problems when signing influencers. RealCap provides a professional screenshot verification solution to help agencies accurately evaluate influencer real value and avoid financial losses from signing fraud.

## Industry Pain Points: Influencer Signing Fraud

MCN (Multi-Channel Network) agencies serve as the bridge between content creators and commercial brands, needing to accurately evaluate commercial value when signing influencers. However, data fraud is becoming an industry-wide phenomenon.

### Fraud Status Data

| Issue | Data | Impact |
|-------|------|--------|
| Income screenshot forgery rate | 40% show suspicious signs | Overvalued contracts |
| Annual fraud losses | Over $300 million | Direct financial losses |
| Engagement data fraud | 35% show anomalies | Poor brand collaboration results |
| MCN lack verification mechanism | 75% have no effective verification | Weak risk control capability |

### Common Fraud Methods

Influencers may use the following methods to forge data when signing:

**Income Screenshot Fraud:**

- Using AI to generate complete platform income interfaces
- Photoshop modifying income numbers in real screenshots
- Using other creators' real screenshots impersonating
- Forging platform-sent "income notification" emails

**Engagement Data Fraud:**

- Purchasing fake followers and engagement
- Using AI to generate fake engagement curve charts
- Forging brand collaboration data and conversion rates
- Modifying engagement metrics in real data screenshots

**Identity Fraud:**

- Impersonating hot account owners
- Forging collaboration records with famous brands
- Fabricating non-existent collaboration cases

### Limitations of Traditional Verification

MCN agencies typically use the following methods to verify influencer data:

| Verification Method | Problem |
|---------------------|---------|
| Manual screenshot viewing | Cannot detect AI forgery, accuracy below 60% |
| Asking income details | Fraudsters often well-prepared to respond |
| Referencing third-party platform data | Data delayed, incomplete, possibly manipulated |
| Small-scale trial collaboration | High cost, fraudsters may perform normally in short-term |
| Requesting backend login | Influencer may refuse, privacy concerns |

These methods are inadequate against AI-enhanced fraud.

## FAQ

### Q: Will influencers resist verification?

Most influencers can accept verification requirements, especially when their data is genuine. Recommendations:

- Clearly state verification is agency standard process
- Explain verification protects honest influencers' interests
- Provide simple verification operation guidance
- Maintain caution for influencers refusing verification

### Q: Will verification affect signing efficiency?

恰恰相反, verification improves signing efficiency:

- Reduces manual review time
- Quickly identifies high-risk signings
- Avoids time consumption from subsequent dispute handling

### Q: Does verification failure definitely mean fraud?

Not necessarily. Verification failure may be caused by multiple reasons:

- Influencer didn't correctly use RealCap to capture screenshot
- Technical issues causing verification anomaly
- Platform interface update causing recognition deviation

Recommend manual review for verification failure cases, not direct rejection.

### Q: How to handle already signed influencers?

Recommendations:

- Introduce verification during renewal
- Trace verification for signed influencers with abnormal performance
- Gradually incorporate verification into renewal standard process
